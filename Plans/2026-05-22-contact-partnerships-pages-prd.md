# Contact & Partnerships Pages — Design Brief

## Context

**Site:** growthmindsetparenting.com
**Brand:** Growth Mindset Parenting — Sean Kane, parenting educator focused on middle schoolers (ages 10–14)
**Brand tone:** Warm, credible, approachable. Educational without being clinical. Feels like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook.
**Design language:** Clean, modern. Emerald green as primary brand color. Geist Sans font. White and light green backgrounds. Rounded corners, soft shadows.
**Audience split:** The site serves two completely different audiences, which is why these are two separate pages — not one combined contact page.

> [!info] Why Two Pages
> **Contact** = parents seeking help, coaching interest, general questions. Emotional, personal, sometimes urgent.
> **Partnerships** = brands, PR agencies, event planners, school administrators. Professional, evaluating fit, looking for an organized creator.
> Combining these sends the wrong signal to both audiences.

---

## Page 1: Contact

### Purpose
Convert warm inbound traffic (parents who already follow Sean or heard about him) into coaching leads and email subscribers. Also provide a clear path for general questions.

### Primary audience
- Parents of middle schoolers (primarily moms, 35–45) with a specific question or problem
- Parents interested in 1-on-1 coaching with Sean
- People who discovered Sean on TikTok or Instagram and want to go deeper

### Conversion goals
1. Coaching inquiry submitted (highest value)
2. General question answered → builds relationship
3. Email list signup if not ready to reach out

### Design direction
Warm and human. This page should feel like reaching out to a real person who will actually read your message — not submitting a ticket. Sean's face should be visible. The tone is "I'm here, tell me what's going on."

---

### Sections

#### Hero
- Headline: Something warm and direct. Not "Get in Touch" (too generic). Something like "Sean reads every message" or "Let's talk about your kid."
- Subhead: 1–2 sentences setting expectation. He responds personally within 1–2 business days.
- No CTA button needed — the form is below the fold immediately.

#### Contact Form
Simple, minimal. 4–5 fields max.

| Field | Type | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Name | Text | Required |
| Email | Email | Required |
| What brings you here? | Select | Options: Interested in coaching / General question / Something else |
| Message | Textarea | Required |

The inquiry type dropdown is important — it helps Sean prioritize coaching leads without adding friction for general questions.

**Submit button:** "Send to Sean" — not "Submit" or "Send Message." More personal.

**Post-submit state:** Clear success message. Reinforce that Sean reads it personally. No generic "your message has been received."

#### Sean's Photo + Personal Note
Alongside or below the form. A photo of Sean (editorial, natural — not posed stock-style) with a short human note: something like "I started this because I remember how lost I felt when my oldest hit middle school. Whatever you're dealing with — I want to help." 1–3 sentences. His voice, not marketing copy.

> [!tip] Design note
> His face is a brand asset. It converts. Prioritize a warm, real photo — not a headshot. Think: sitting at a table, outdoors, candid.

#### Other Ways to Connect
Secondary section. 3–4 cards with real links (not decorative). Use SVG icons, not emojis.

- **Instagram:** @growth.mindset.parenting (100K followers — lead with this)
- **TikTok:** @growth.mindset.parenting (38K followers)
- **Email:** hello@growthmindsetparenting.com
- **Newsletter:** Weekly strategies every Tuesday — link to signup

#### FAQ
Accordion style. 4–5 questions covering what parents actually ask — real questions, real answers. **Sean needs to provide these** — they should come from the most common things he gets asked via DM or email, not generic placeholders.

> [!warning] To do before design
> Ask Sean: what are the 4–5 questions parents ask you most often? Those become the FAQs. Do not use generic questions like "how quickly do you respond."

---

### What NOT to include
- No social proof stats bar (that belongs on the Partnerships page, not this one — it changes the tone)
- No brand logos or media mentions (wrong audience)
- No course upsell (this is a help page, not a sales page)
- No speaking/partnership mentions (those have their own page)

## Page 2: Partnerships

### Purpose
Signal to brands, event planners, and schools that Sean is open for business and worth reaching out to. This page needs to establish credibility and make inquiry frictionless — even before Sean has an extensive track record.

### Primary audience
- **Brands:** Products aligned with parenting, education, child development, family wellness. Looking for authentic creators with engaged audiences in the parent demographic.
- **Podcast hosts:** Looking for knowledgeable, articulate guests on parenting, adolescent development, education, or family topics. This is a priority opportunity — lean into it.
- **Event planners / PTAs / schools:** Booking speakers for parent nights, conferences, professional development.
- **Corporate HR / L&D teams:** Companies interested in bringing Sean in to speak to employees who are parents of middle schoolers — a niche but real opportunity.
- **Media:** Press, features, expert commentary.

### Conversion goals
1. Brand partnership inquiry submitted
2. Podcast guest inquiry submitted (priority — high volume opportunity)
3. Speaking inquiry submitted (live events, corporate, schools)
4. Credibility established even if visitor doesn't reach out (word-of-mouth, future outreach)

### Design direction
Professional and confident — but still Sean's voice. This is not a sterile corporate page. It should feel like a creator who takes business seriously without losing the warmth that makes him credible with parents. Think: the professionalism of a media kit combined with the personality of his content.

---

### Sections

#### Hero
- Headline: Something that signals openness and relevance. "Partner With Growth Mindset Parenting" or "Let's Build Something Together."
- Subhead: 2–3 sentences establishing why brands want Sean's audience. The middle school parent demographic is underserved and highly engaged. His audience trusts his recommendations because his content earns that trust first.
- No gimmicks. Clean and direct.

#### Audience Stats
A clean stats row. Not a full media kit — just the numbers that matter to a brand manager.

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| TikTok followers | 38K+ |
| Instagram followers | 100K |
| Instagram monthly views | 3.1M+ |
| Newsletter subscribers | 3K+ |
| Audience | Parents of middle schoolers, primarily moms 35–45 |

> [!note]
> These numbers will grow. Design this section to be easy to update.

#### Brand Partnerships
Subsection with its own heading. 2–3 sentences describing what Sean looks for in a brand partner: alignment with his values (education, growth, honesty with kids), products his audience would genuinely use, no hard sell. Then a simple inquiry form or email.

**What to include in the form:**
- Name + company
- Website or social handle
- Brief description of the partnership idea
- Email

**Framing note:** Sean hasn't done paid partnerships yet. The copy should be written as "we're selective about who we work with" rather than "we've worked with X, Y, Z." Selectivity is a proxy for credibility when you don't have a track record yet.

#### Podcast Appearances
Dedicated subsection — this is a priority opportunity and should feel prominent, not buried under speaking.

Sean is an ideal podcast guest: articulate, research-backed, covers an underserved niche (middle school parenting), and brings a built-in audience. Podcast hosts in the parenting, education, family wellness, and personal development spaces are the primary targets.

**Include:**
- 2–3 sentences on what Sean brings as a guest (unique angle, audience overlap, credibility)
- 3–4 sample topics/angles a host could pitch their audience (see below)
- A note that Sean is available for virtual recordings
- Simple inquiry prompt: "Hosting a podcast? Tell us about your show and audience."

**Sample podcast topics (draft — Sean should refine):**
1. **Why middle school breaks the parent-child relationship** — and what to do before it's too late
2. **The neuroscience of the 12-year-old brain** — what parents get wrong and what actually works
3. **Growth mindset isn't just for kids** — how parents need to change first
4. **Sibling cruelty in the tween years** — why it happens and how to stop it without making it worse

#### Speaking
Subsection. Sean is available for live and virtual events: parent nights, PTA events, school programs, and corporate employee sessions.

**Corporate note:** Companies with large employee bases increasingly book parenting educators for employee resource groups and wellness programming. Middle school is a peak stress period for working parents — this is a real and growing market. Frame it as available without over-indexing on it.

**Include:**
- 2–3 speaking topics with 1-sentence descriptions (see below)
- Brief note on format: keynote, workshop, Q&A panel, virtual or in-person
- Audiences: parents, educators, school staff, corporate employees

**Speaking topics (draft — Sean should refine):**
1. **The Middle School Brain** — What's actually happening neurologically at 10–14, and why your parenting instincts probably aren't helping
2. **From Manager to Coach** — How to shift your parenting approach before your kid shuts you out completely
3. **Raising Kids Who Can Handle Hard Things** — The research on resilience, growth mindset, and why protecting kids from struggle backfires

#### Inquiry Form
One unified form for both partnership types, with a dropdown to indicate type. Keep it short — brands and event planners won't fill out a long form.

| Field | Type |
|-------|------|
| Name | Text |
| Company / Organization | Text |
| Inquiry type | Select: Brand Partnership / Podcast Guest / Speaking (Live Event) / Corporate Speaking / Media / Other |
| Website or social | Text |
| Tell us about the opportunity | Textarea |
| Email | Email |

**Submit copy:** "Send Inquiry" — professional, neutral.

**Post-submit:** Simple confirmation. Someone will be in touch within 3–5 business days (different SLA than the contact page — these are business inquiries).

---

### What NOT to include
- No coaching inquiry path (that's on the Contact page)
- No parent-facing FAQ
- No newsletter signup as the primary CTA (this audience doesn't want Sean's parenting tips)
- No inflated or unverifiable claims

---

## Navigation

Both pages should live in the main nav:

`About · Courses · Contact · Partnerships`

"Partnerships" in the nav is a signal to the business world on its own — it tells any brand or event planner who lands on the homepage that Sean is open to this kind of work.

---

## Shared Design Rules

- SVG icons only — no emojis
- `cursor-pointer` on all clickable elements
- Minimum 44px touch targets on mobile
- Form inputs have visible labels (not placeholder-only)
- Success states use `role="alert"` for accessibility
- Focus states visible for keyboard navigation
- Responsive at 375px, 768px, 1024px, 1440px
- Emerald brand color: `#059669` (emerald-600)
